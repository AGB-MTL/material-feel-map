The Material Feel Map

Sensation becomes feeling becomes meaning, through combination. Touch is made of a few independent dimensions. Set how a material sits on each, and watch what the combination comes to mean.

Step 1
How does it feel to the hand?
Six independent dimensions. A material can be soft and cool, or soft and warm. Set each one freely.
Try soft, then switch between warm and cool. The meaning changes completely.
Step 2
What does it come to mean?
Each reading is a hypothesis: a combination of sensations, the feeling it tends to stir, and the meaning it tends to carry.
Browse all readings, or pick sensations to filter.
How this becomes a benchmark. The dimensions are the measurable inputs; the readings are hypotheses about how their combinations map to human response. Some are strong (soft + warm → comforting). Some are contested (rough can read as natural craft or as unfinished). Many combinations have no established reading yet. SENSUS-Bench tests which mappings hold, for whom, and how well models predict them.
